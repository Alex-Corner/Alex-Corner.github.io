Alex Corner

Senior Lecturer

Teaching

I was first employed as a lecturer at Sheffield Hallam University in September 2016 and have been a Senior Lecturer since September 2019. From 2017 to 2022 I was the First Year Tutor for the BSc Mathematics course and since 2019 I have acted as an LGBT+ Tutor for both the BSc Mathematics and BSc Physics courses.

I previously worked as an Assistant Demonstrator, Lead Demonstrator, and Marking Assistant at the University of Sheffield, as well as being a Visiting Sessional Tutor at the International College of the University of Sheffield.

I will be teaching on the following modules in the 2025/26 academic year:

  • Fundamentals of Mathematics and Computer Architectures
    • first-year discrete mathematics for computer scientists
  • Introduction to Cyber Security, Mathematics, and Cryptography
    • first-year discrete mathematics and cryptography for cyber security students
  • Mathematical and Statistical Methods
    • first-year linear algebra, statistics, and probability for mathematicians
  • Maths for Foundation Computing
    • foundation level mathematics for students progressing onto computing degrees

I will also be supervising final year computer science and software engineering students in their development projects, along with masters level data analytics apprenticeship students.
